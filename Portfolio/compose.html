What is Composting?

Composting is the natural process of recycling organic matter, such as food scraps and yard waste, into a valuable fertilizer that can enrich soil and support plant growth.

Step-by-Step Guide to Composting

Step 1: Gather Your Materials

Collect kitchen scraps like vegetable peels, fruit cores, and coffee grounds. Avoid meat and dairy products.

Step 2: Create a Compost Bin

Set up a compost bin or pile in a well-drained area. Ensure it has good airflow.

Step 3: Layer Your Materials

Alternate layers of "greens" (nitrogen-rich materials) and "browns" (carbon-rich materials) for a balanced mix.

Step 4: Maintain Moisture

Keep the compost pile moist but not soggy. Water it if it feels dry.

Step 5: Turn the Pile

Turn your compost pile every few weeks to aerate it and speed up decomposition.

Step 6: Harvest Your Compost

After a few months, your compost will be ready. It should be dark, crumbly, and smell earthy.

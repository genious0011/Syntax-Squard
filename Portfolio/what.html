What to compost orWhat Not to COMPOST

What to compost

composting leftover food or kitchen waste, it's super important to know what can go into your compost pile and what should stay out to keep it healthy, odor-free, and pest-free. Here's a simple matcher guide:>

  • Fruit and vegetable scraps (banana peels, apple cores, etc.)

  • Coffee grounds and filters

  • Tea bags (check if they're plastic-free)

  • Eggshells (crushed)

  • Cooked plain rice and pasta (small amounts, no sauce or oil)

  • Bread (small pieces, avoid moldy bits in indoor composters)

  • Spoiled fruits or veggies

  • Used paper napkins/towels (unbleached, no chemicals)

  • Cereal or oatmeal (plain, no dairy or sugar)

What not to compost

  • Meat, fish, or bones

  • Dairy (milk, cheese, yogurt)

  • Oily or greasy food

  • Sauces, dressings, or gravies

  • Baked goods with frosting or sugar

  • Plastic, foil, or food wrappers

  • Citrus peels (in large amounts â€“ slow to break down)

  • Onion and garlic (can slow microbial activity)

  • Pet waste (from carnivores)

  • Diseased plants
